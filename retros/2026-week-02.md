## Retrospective: Start / Stop / Continue

### START
- **Writing acceptance criteria before every prompt** — This prevented scope creep and made verification objective.
- **Using the pre-flight checklist** (Role + Input + Output + Acceptance) — It consistently produced better results than ad-hoc prompts.
- **Reading SKILL.md files end-to-end** — Understanding how Skills are structured taught me more than just invoking them.

### STOP
- **Assuming the agent will get it right on the first try** — I learned to treat it like a junior colleague: give clear instructions, review, give feedback, iterate.
- **Skipping the "S" in K-S-T-A-R** — Early on, I jumped straight from K to T without describing the situation. This led to vague tasks and unclear outputs.
- **Over-prompting** — I caught myself re-prompting when a small steering adjustment would have been more efficient (and saved tokens).

### CONTINUE
- **The K-S-T-A-R loop** — Each R becomes new K, making the next cycle faster. This is the core learning mechanism.
- **Committing everything** — The git history became a learning journal I can look back on.
- **Partner swap / peer review** — Reading someone else's coordinate card on Day 8 gave me ideas I wouldn't have thought of alone.
